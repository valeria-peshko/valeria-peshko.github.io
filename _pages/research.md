## You Never Give Me Your Money: Barriers to Female Entrepreneurship in the Russian Empire, 1892-1914.

*European Review of Economic History, forthcoming*

This paper examines gender disparities in access to entrepreneurship in the late Russian Empire using a dataset of more than 14,000 corporations and partnerships from 1892, 1904, and 1914. Women constitute a small but persistent share of corporate founders and an increasing share of partners. The analysis reveals notable differences in firm capitalization: companies with female founders are comparable to fully male-owned corporations but exhibit substantially lower capitalization in partnerships, even after controlling for location, industry, and other characteristics. I interpret these findings as indicating the presence of a gender‑based barrier to incorporation, which in turn leads to positive selection. In contrast, the lower capitalization of partnerships appears more consistent with limited access to financial markets than with restrictions arising from married women’s property rights. Patterns in the demographic composition of co‑owners further indicate gendered differences in social capital and business networks.
